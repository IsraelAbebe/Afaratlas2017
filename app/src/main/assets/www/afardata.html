Livelihood Profile

The Region’s livelihood baseline were prepared during 17 January 2017 to 11 February 2017.Different NGOs, private companies, and GOs Participated in the preparation.Thus, Pastoralism is the dominant livelihood system in the region. Specifically, Camel dominant pastoralism is the dominant.Transhumance is the norm. Agro-pastoralism is practiced where irrigation is possible.Based on this study, Afar Region have 8 livelihood zones.These zones are homogeneous area, where people share broadly similar production and access to markets.The method consider, HEA, Agro-ecology and Geography

Meteorology

There are minimum weather stations in the region (most of them are non-functional). Only 17 stations are class 1 and most of them are class 3&4 (68%). Class 1 Stns measures real time all weather parameters. Class 3 and 4 measures minimum weather parameters in a day. Fuka et al. Hydrography

The region has good water resources potential.Different lakes, rivers and wetlands exist.Detail hydrological morphometric analysis at watershed level is prepared. Each drainage lines is digitized and measured. Drainage network, texture and relief is computed.

  • 2 River Basins
  • 122 Watersheds within the region
  • 31 Lakes
  • Lake Abby is the largest, Afdera Saline Lake is 2nd.

Soil
